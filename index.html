HISTORY:

Don Macchiatos was founded in Cebu, Philippines, in October 2022 by Nickie San Juan, a former call center agent who experienced financial difficulty affording coffee as a student, aiming to provide affordable and quality coffee (around 39 pesos). They grew from a local phenomenon to a widely recognized brand by focusing on accessibility and community, becoming known as Cebu's first P39 coffee. The brand officially started in October 2022, building on success from their prior venture, Pure Leaf Milktea. Their affordable, viral success quickly spread across Cebu and other Philippine cities, inspiring many other budget coffee vendors.

LIFE OF THE OWNER:

The life of Don Macchiatos' owner features a journey of passion, affordability, and community, led by founders Ariel Alegado( general manager ) and Nickie San Juan ( co-founder ), who started with a vision to make quality coffee accessible (P39 in Cebu). They faced challenges but remained dedicated to their dream, inspired by college struggles to offer quality drinks for everyone, growing from a small stall to a national brand supported by endorsements (like Daniel Padilla) and a focus on local support, especially for farmers and aspiring entrepreneurs. They started with a small corner stall in Cebu. In essence, their story is about turning financial hardship and a love for coffee into a successful, inclusive brand that champions local support and accessibility for all.

SUCCESS STORY OF THE BUSINESS:

The success story of Don Macchiatos is rooted in a mission to provide quality, accessible coffee options for everyone. Founded by Nickie San Juan and her partner Ariel Alegado, the brand has grown rapidly from a small corner stall in Cebu to a nationwide franchise with over 850 outlets. For two consecutive years (2023 and 2024), Don Macchiatos was named the Most Trusted Coffee @39 by the Global Management Excellence Award. Their story serves as an inspiration for aspiring entrepreneurs, proving that passion and a commitment to serving the community can lead to remarkable success.

CHALLENGE MET BY THE OWNER:

A primary challenge was finding high-quality ingredients that would allow them to sell coffee at the low price point of P39 and only a few loyal customers. They had to find their place in an already competitive coffee market, especially with the rise of other businesses inspired by their affordable coffee concept. As the business rapidly expanded, they occasionally ran out of stock, leading to potential delays in delivery from their main base in Cebu. Nickie San Juan was personally motivated to start the business due to her own financial constraints as a student, highlighting the initial difficulty in funding and managing a new venture. They had to deal diverse customer opinions, including some negative feedback regarding quality or false advertising, addressing these issues while maintaining their brand reputation.

How will the owner see their business 10 years from now.

In ten years, Don Macchiatos aims to become a nationwide leader in affordable, high-quality coffee in the Philippines and explore global expansion. It plans to expand across Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao, turning many branches into cozy cafe-style spaces for studying, working, and relaxing. The brand will continue to innovate its menu, support communities, and maintain efficient operations to ensure long-term success. Ultimately, Don Macchiatos wants to be a trusted and well-loved brand that makes quality coffee accessible to everyone.

How did the Philippine government help the business grow?

The Philippine government helps businesses grow through offering tax incentives, investing heavily in infrastructure, providing financial aid (loans), and fostering public-private partnerships to create a favorable, competitive environment for both local and foreign investment, boosting jobs and economic activity. These measures aim to attract investment, empower small businesses, and generate quality jobs for inclusive economic growth.
